Park Analysis of Benton County

Have you ever wondered where your parks are in Benton County?, or maybe just in the city of Corvallis. This story map will tell you about those different parks, as well as some interesting characteristics and features of those parks



Story Map Created by Leith Seymour

Park and Open Space Types


This view shows the different tpyes of land that the parks in Benton County are

For this map the green areas are natural spaces, red areas and recreational areas, and yellow area within school grounds.

Additionally parks for the city of corvallis which aren't included in those designations are marked blue, and Golf courses in town have been indicated with a marker

In this area you checkerboard pattern in the landscape, to read more about this click the image below

Park and Open Space Management


In the previous view we could see many of the same spaces as here, but not who managed those spaces.

Here you can see that Green spaces are federally managed(BLM,ODFW,etc), Red spaces area managed by the county, and Blue again by the city of Corvallis.

Also you can see there is an orange area. This outlines the Mcdunn forest area that is managed by Oregonstate University. This area includes the McDonald and Dunn forests

To see more information about it click the image below

City Parks Playgrounds


Now Zoomed in on Corvallis we can see just the city parks

This data view shows which parks are considered to have a playground with green being having one and red not having one

As you can see, there is only one park with a play ground under the cities jurdisdiction, Willamette park

Don't forgot that this doesn't include playgrounds on school grounds or in county managed parks, but just in City run parks

click the image for the City of Corvallis page on Willamette Park

City Parks Dogs


Have you ever wanted to know which parks or open spaces you can take your faithful companion, or maybe somewhere to get away from dogs for a day

This Map shows all the parks and open spaces in the city which allow dogs in green, and all those that don't in red

The area you see in red there is the Caldwell Natural Area Park

Hopefully you have a better understanding of the parks in Benton County, the city of Corvallis, and what you can do at them.
